<p class="page-intro">Each Sunday we offer group road rides led by our members over varying distances and speeds to ensure everyone can ride at their level. Every other Sunday during spring/summer we also run a slower G4 ride.</p>

<div class="callout callout-danger">
<strong>All new members must ride with G3 for their first ride.</strong> Helmets are mandatory on all rides.
</div>

Ride and route details are published on our [Facebook page](https://www.facebook.com/groups/LBRCC/) towards the end of each week.

**Meeting point:** The Propellor monument opposite Dorvics at the bottom of Leighton Buzzard High Street for an **8:30am** departure. This may change due to weather — check the Facebook ride post.

The ride speeds and distances are for reference and can change on the day due to group size, terrain, weather and ability.

<div class="rules-grid" markdown="1">

<div class="rule-section" markdown="1">
## 🟢 Group 4 (G4)

Not just for beginners — also for those looking for a shorter distance at a pace to suit all riders. Speed from **10mph** with no-one left behind. Runs on alternating Sundays throughout spring and summer.
</div>

<div class="rule-section" markdown="1">
## 🔵 Group 3 (G3)

**30–40 miles** at up to **14mph** (could be faster in agreement with the group). Rides at the pace of the slowest rider — no-one is dropped. All new riders start here.
</div>

<div class="rule-section" markdown="1">
## 🟠 Group 2 (G2)

**40–50 miles** at **15–17mph+** in agreement with the group. Opportunity to change groups at the coffee stop. Regular rotations on the front. Often split into two groups on high-turnout days.
</div>

<div class="rule-section" markdown="1">
## 🔴 Group 1 (G1)

**50–70 miles** at **17–19mph+** in agreement with the group. Rides as a tight group practising regular rotations.
</div>

</div>

## 🗓️ Ad-Hoc Rides

Additional rides on some Saturday mornings, Sunday afternoons, and evenings throughout the week. Organised by individual members — look out for posts on Facebook. These may have different formats (hill climbing, training, etc.) so check with the organiser.
